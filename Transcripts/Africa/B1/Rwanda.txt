Sam: That is a great choice, Alex. Rwanda is a fascinating place. It is located in East-Central Africa. It is a small, landlocked country, which means it has no coast or ocean. It is surrounded by four neighbors: Uganda, Tanzania, Burundi, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Alex: People often call it the Land of a Thousand Hills. Why is that, Sam?

Sam: Well, if you look at a map or visit the country, you will see why. It is full of beautiful green mountains and hills. It is very green and very clean. In fact, the capital city, Kigali, is known as one of the cleanest cities in the whole world.

Alex: That sounds wonderful. But we cannot talk about Rwanda without mentioning its history. It has a very sad and difficult past, doesn’t it?

Sam: Yes, it does. In 1994, Rwanda experienced a terrible genocide. In only 100 days, about 800,000 people were killed because of ethnic conflicts. It was a dark time that the world will never forget. However, what is amazing is how the country has recovered since then. They have worked very hard on peace and reconciliation.

Alex: It is truly remarkable how they moved forward. Let’s talk about the people today. What languages do they speak in Rwanda?

Sam: They are very lucky because most people speak Kinyarwanda, which is the national language. But because of their history and international connections, they also use English, French, and Swahili. If you are a tourist who speaks English, you will find it very easy to communicate there.

Alex: That leads me to a big question. Is Rwanda a democracy or a dictatorship? I hear different things in the news.

Sam: That is a complicated topic. The president, Paul Kagame, has been in power for a long time. Supporters say he is a hero because he brought stability, safety, and economic growth after the genocide. But critics say there is not enough freedom for the political opposition. So, some call it a "managed democracy," while others think it is more like a dictatorship.

Alex: I see. And what about the economy? Is Rwanda a rich or a poor country?

Sam: Most experts would say it is still a developing country. Many people still work in farming and live on a low income. However, the economy is growing very fast. They are investing a lot in technology and tourism. They want to become the "Singapore of Africa."

Alex: Speaking of tourism, is it worth visiting? And more importantly, is it safe?

Sam: It is definitely worth visiting! The biggest attraction is the mountain gorillas. People travel from all over the world to hike into the forest and see them. It is a unique experience. Regarding safety, Rwanda is actually one of the safest countries in Africa. You can walk around the capital at night and feel very secure.

Alex: What about their relationships with other countries? I heard they have some problems with their neighbors.

Sam: It’s a mix. Rwanda has very strong ties with Western countries like the UK and the United States. They receive a lot of help and investment from them. However, relations with their neighbor, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, are often very tense. There are many arguments about borders and security in that region.

Alex: It sounds like a country of contrasts. It has a tragic history but a very bright and modern future.

Sam: Exactly. It is a place that teaches us about resilience. They took a broken country and turned it into a organized, clean, and safe nation. Even if you don't agree with their politics, you have to respect their progress.
