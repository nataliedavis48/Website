Sam: Well, the policy officially started around 1980. Before that, in the 1950s and 60s, China’s population was growing very fast. The government was scared that they would not be able to feed everyone. They were worried about poverty and a lack of natural resources like water and land.

Alex: So, the leaders thought the best solution was to limit the number of children each family could have?

Sam: Exactly. They believed that if the population stopped growing so quickly, the country could become wealthy and modern more fast. They wanted to make sure there were enough jobs and schools for everyone.

Sam: It was very difficult for them. They often had to pay very large fines, which are sums of money paid as a punishment. Sometimes people even lost their jobs or faced other social problems. The government used many ways to make sure people followed the law.

Sam: We definitely do. One of the biggest issues now is the "aging population." Because there were fewer births for about 35 years, China now has many elderly people and not enough young workers to support them or pay for their healthcare.

Alex: I’ve also heard about the "Little Emperor" effect. Can you explain that to our listeners?

Sam: Sure! Since many children grew up without any brothers or sisters, they received all the attention and money from their two parents and four grandparents. Some people say this made that generation a bit spoiled or lonely.

Alex: Another serious consequence was the gender gap. Why did that happen?

Sam: Traditionally, many families preferred to have a son to carry on the family name. This led to a situation where many more boys were born than girls. Today, there are millions more men than women in China, which makes it very hard for many men to find a partner and get married.

Sam: They started changing it slowly as they saw these problems growing. In 2016, they ended the One-Child Policy and allowed couples to have two children. Then, in 2021, the policy changed again. Now, couples can have three children.
