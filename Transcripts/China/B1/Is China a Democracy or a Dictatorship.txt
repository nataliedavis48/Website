Sam: Not in the way we usually think. The leaders are chosen from within the Party. Because one group has all the power and there is no opposition, most political experts call China an authoritarian state or a dictatorship. One party makes all the big rules, and you cannot vote them out.

Sam: It comes down to control and consequences. The Chinese government uses a very advanced system of censorship. They have thousands of people and smart computers monitoring the internet every second.

Alex: I’ve heard of the "Great Firewall." Does that stop people from seeing certain things?

Sam: Yes. It blocks websites like Google, or news sites that criticize China. But it also works inside the country. If you post a message on social media criticizing a leader, that post will likely be deleted within minutes. If someone keeps doing it, they might lose their job or even be arrested by the police.

Alex: That sounds very stressful. So, people just stay quiet to stay safe?

Sam: Most people do. They focus on their lives, their families, and their jobs. They know that speaking out against the Party can lead to serious trouble. This creates a "culture of silence" regarding politics.

Alex: It’s a huge contrast because, on the other hand, China looks so modern and rich. This brings me to my next question: Is it a communist country or a capitalist one?

Sam: This is the most confusing part! Officially, China is a communist country. In theory, communism means the government owns everything and everyone is equal. But if you walk through cities like Shanghai or Shenzhen, it looks like the most capitalist place on Earth.

Alex: Right! There are luxury shops, massive private companies like Alibaba, and more billionaires than almost anywhere else. How is that communist?

Sam: Well, in the late 1970s, China realized that pure communism wasn't working well for the economy. They started a "market economy." They allowed people to start businesses and make profits. They call this "Socialism with Chinese Characteristics."

Alex: So, it’s a mix?

Sam: Precisely. It is a "hybrid" system. It uses capitalist tools to grow the economy, but the Communist Party still keeps total control over the land, the banks, and the law.
