This was an official document that declared all enslaved people in the Confederate states to be free. It changed the purpose of the war. It was no longer just about keeping the country together; it was now a fight for human freedom.

Sam: That same year, 1863, saw the Battle of Gettysburg. This is often called the turning point of the war. The Union won a major victory there, and the Confederacy never really recovered after that. Shortly after the battle, Lincoln gave his famous Gettysburg Address, a short speech that reminded everyone why democracy was worth fighting for.
