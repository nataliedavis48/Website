Sam: It is because of their history. The island was a center for trade for many hundreds of years. The Spanish were there first, then the Dutch took control. Because they are close to South America, they learn Spanish. Because of tourism, they learn English.

Alex: And what about Papiamentu? Where does that language come from?

Sam: Papiamentu is a beautiful mix. it has words from Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch, and even African languages. It started because people from different cultures needed to talk to each other. Now, it is the heart of their culture.
