Hello, and welcome to our podcast. I'm your host, Lauren. Today, we're talking about our daily habits. We often think habits are for our bodies—like eating healthy or exercising. But did you know that small daily habits are also very powerful for our minds? They can help us feel happier, more focused, and less stressed in our daily lives.
An interesting idea comes from a writer named Seth Gillihan. He talks about a method called Mindful Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. That name sounds complicated, but the idea is simple. It has three main parts that help us understand and gently change our thoughts and feelings.
First, there is 'mindfulness'. Mindfulness simply means paying attention to the present moment. It's about noticing what is happening right now, without judging it. For example, you can take a moment to just focus on your breathing. This simple action helps to calm your mind when it feels busy or worried.
The second part is 'cognitive'. This is about our thoughts. We all have negative thoughts sometimes, like "I am not good enough." The cognitive part helps us notice these thoughts and then ask, "Is this thought really true? Is there a more helpful way to see this?" It's about learning to question our unhelpful thoughts.
And the third part is 'behavior'. This is about the small actions we take every day. We don't need to make big changes all at once. Taking just one small, positive step is a powerful way to change how we feel. Even listening to one happy song can make a real difference.
The key to making these habits stick is consistency. It's better to practice for just one minute every day than for 30 minutes once a week. When you do something consistently, your brain starts to build new pathways. It's like building a new road. The more you travel on it, the smoother and more established it becomes. So, even on days when you don't feel like it, try to do your small habit for just a minute. This consistency is what builds lasting change.
These small daily exercises can help us change mental patterns that are not useful, like being too critical of ourselves. Self-criticism is when we are very hard on ourselves. By practicing a small, kind action each day—like saying one nice thing to yourself—we can learn to be a better friend to ourselves.
Daily rituals are also very important. A ritual is a small habit that you do regularly, like making a warm cup of tea in the afternoon or reading for ten minutes before bed. These small rituals give our day a comfortable structure. They help reduce stress because they are familiar and calming.
So, the main message is this: managing stress and feeling better is possible for everyone. It is not about making huge, difficult changes. It is about the small, easy practices we choose to do every day. These small habits add up over time and create a big, positive change in our lives. Remember, it is completely okay to have difficult days. Everyone struggles sometimes. The goal is not to be perfect. The goal is to be gentle and patient with yourself, and to keep trying.
