Sam: And Mumbai used to be called Bombay, right?

Alex: Exactly. The name is a combination of "Bombay" and "Hollywood." While India has many other huge film industries in languages like Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam, Bollywood is the one that has the biggest global reach and is the most recognized internationally.

Sam: It’s amazing how the name stuck. So, how far back does this history go? Did it start around the same time as cinema in the West?

Alex: Pretty much! The journey of Indian cinema began over a hundred years ago. The very first Indian feature film was a silent movie called "Raja Harishchandra," which was released in 1913. It was based on ancient Indian legends. But the real turning point happened in 1931 with a film called "Alam Ara."

Sam: Was that the first one with sound?

Alex: Yes, it was the first "talkie." And more importantly, it was the film that introduced song and dance to the screen. That decision changed everything and created the unique style we see today. By the 1950s, which people often call the "Golden Age" of Indian cinema, Bollywood was already producing masterpieces that were being watched in places like the Soviet Union and the Middle East.

Sam: That leads me to my next question. What makes a Bollywood movie so special? If you watch a movie from Hollywood or Europe, it usually follows a specific genre, like a thriller or a romantic comedy. But Bollywood seems to do everything at once.

Alex: That is exactly what makes it unique! There is a specific term for this: "Masala" films. Just like the spice mix used in Indian cooking, these movies are a blend of everything. In one single three-hour movie, you will get action, deep drama, slapstick comedy, and a beautiful romance. 

Sam: And we can’t forget the musical numbers. I’ve always wondered, do the actors actually sing those songs?

Alex: Usually, they don’t. This is another fascinating part of the industry. They use "playback singers." These are professional singers who record the songs in a studio, and then the actors lip-sync and dance to them on set. Some of these singers are just as famous as the movie stars themselves.

Sam: It’s like a giant spectacle. I’ve also noticed that these movies are quite long. I remember trying to watch one and being surprised that it didn't end after two hours.

Alex: Most Bollywood films are around three hours long. Because of the length, every movie has an "intermission"—a short break in the middle so the audience can get food or stretch their legs. It’s designed to be a full afternoon or evening of entertainment. It’s not just a movie; it’s an event for the whole family.

Sam: So, how big is the industry today? I’ve heard some people say it’s even bigger than Hollywood.

Alex: In terms of the number of films produced, yes, it is massive. India as a whole produces between 1,500 and 2,000 films every year, and Bollywood is the largest contributor to that. In terms of ticket sales, Bollywood often sells more tickets globally than Hollywood does, because the audience in India is so huge and the diaspora living abroad is very loyal to the films.

Sam: It’s incredible how much influence it has. I see Bollywood-style dancing in fitness classes and even in Western music videos now.

Alex: It really is a cultural phenomenon. It influences fashion, wedding traditions, and even the way people speak. When a big movie comes out, the songs become the soundtrack for the entire country for months. It’s a very powerful form of storytelling that celebrates emotion and family values.
